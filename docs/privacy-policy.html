Privacy Policy

FlashNote for iOS and watchOS — Last updated February 18, 2026

FlashNote is a fully offline app. Your notes, voice recordings, and personal data never leave your device. We do not collect, store, or transmit any personal information.

Data Storage

All data created in FlashNote is stored locally on your device using Apple's SwiftData framework. This includes:

  • Text notes and captures
  • Voice recordings and transcriptions
  • App preferences and settings

No data is uploaded to any server, cloud service, or third-party platform.

Speech Recognition

FlashNote uses Apple's on-device speech recognition to transcribe voice notes. All speech processing happens entirely on your device. No audio data is sent to Apple or any third party.

Watch Connectivity

If you use FlashNote on Apple Watch, notes are transferred directly between your Watch and iPhone using Apple's WatchConnectivity framework. This transfer happens locally over Bluetooth or Wi-Fi and does not involve any external servers.

Notifications

FlashNote may send local notifications to help you review and reflect on your saved notes. These notifications are scheduled entirely on-device and do not use push notification servers.

Third-Party Services

FlashNote does not integrate with any third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking services. The app contains no third-party SDKs that collect data.

Data Sharing

FlashNote does not share any data with third parties. When you use the export or integration features (e.g., sharing to Reminders, Things, or Obsidian), data is sent directly to those apps on your device using URL schemes. No intermediary servers are involved.

Children's Privacy

FlashNote does not knowingly collect any information from children. Since the app collects no data from any user, it is safe for use by people of all ages.

Changes to This Policy

If we make changes to this privacy policy, the updated version will be posted on this page with a revised date.
